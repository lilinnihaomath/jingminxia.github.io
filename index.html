Research Interest

 During my phd, I worked on scientific computing of elliptic partial differential equations and numerical analysis, particularly focusing on math problems arising from liquid crystals. My phd thesis entitled Computational and Analytical Aspects of Energy Minimisation Problems in Cholesteric, Ferronematic and Smectic Liquid Crystals can be found here.

Currently, I am applying mathematical modelling and scientific computing techniques in the area of oceanic problems. More specifically, I focus on numerical ocean prediction at the current stage and thus have started to use some numerical tools, e.g., FVCOM for finite volume community ocean model and SWAN for simulating waves nearshore.

I mainly code with Python and occasionally Fortran, Matlab and Mathematica. I use firedrake as the finite element library and defcon for the bifurcation analysis.

There are some selected research topics below:
  • Partial differential equations
  • Mathematical modelling and scientific computing
  • Numerical Analysis
  • Bifurcation analysis
  • Liquid crystals
  • Numerical ocean prediction
